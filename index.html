About

LINKKI is a simplified kinetic toy designed by Eun Young Park. Notwithstanding its simplicity, its use is being proven quite versatile as a STEM educational tool, designer's kinetic prototyping tool, and artistic medium. However, the advantage of LINKKI, among others, especially lies in its adaptability and hackability. With basic Fab Lab tools such as laser cutter and affordable materials such as cardboards, parts can be easily fabricated. Through workshops and Maker Faire's, I got to notice the need of affordable teaching kits for motion design. But most of all, when it comes to the experience with LINKKI, the more widely it is shared, the more inspiring it gets. That's why I share the resources. Please enjoy and share your experience!

Material

For a board (9mm-10mm)

  • Cardboard
  • MDF
  • Plywood
  • Plexiglass

For bars and circles (3mm)

  • Plywood
  • MDF
  • Plexiglass (Cardboard is too flexible to make bars)
